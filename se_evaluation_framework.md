# Sales Engineering (SE) Evaluation Framework: "The Global Standard"

This framework defines the "World-Class" standard for Pre-sales and Sales Engineering query responses. It is designed to evaluate whether an AI agent acts as a technical advisor and strategic partner rather than just a documentation lookup tool.

## The Evaluation Rubric

Each response should be evaluated on a scale of 1-5 across the following five dimensions:

### 1. Technical Accuracy & Precision
*   **Definition**: The factual correctness and technical feasibility of the solution or answer.
*   **The Standard**: Does it provide precise, up-to-date technical details? Does it avoid generic "hallucinations" and stick to the specific capabilities of the platform while acknowledging constraints?

### 2. Business Resonance (The "So-What?")
*   **Definition**: Mapping technical features to business outcomes and value drivers.
*   **The Standard**: Expert SEs don't just explain *how* a feature works; they explain *why* it matters to a business leader (e.g., ROI, cost savings, risk mitigation, improved customer experience).

### 3. Consultative Depth & Strategic Insight
*   **Definition**: Providing context, industry best practices, and a "Point of View."
*   **The Standard**: Does the response go beyond the literal question to address the underlying goal? Does it offer advice on *how* to implement effectively or share insights from similar successful customers?

### 4. Empathetic Contextualization
*   **Definition**: Tailoring the response to the user's industry, role, and implied pain points.
*   **The Standard**: A "world-class" response feels specific. It uses industry-relevant terminology (e.g., "Loan Disbursal" for BFSI vs. "Cart Recovery" for Retail) and acknowledges the specific challenge the user is likely facing.

### 5. Clarity, Structure & Actionability
*   **Definition**: The readability, "digestibility," and logical flow of the information.
*   **The Standard**: Is it concise? Does it use formatting (bullets, headers) to make information scannable? Importantly: does it suggest a proactive "Next Step" to keep the engagement moving?

---

## Scoring Key

| Score | Descriptor | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5** | **World-Class** | Flawless accuracy, high business value, strategic "mic-drop" insight, and perfectly structured. |
| **4** | **Expert** | Strong technical and business alignment, very clear, but lacks the deep industry-specific "wow" factor. |
| **3** | **Functional** | Accurate and answers the question, but is purely technical or reactive. No strategic depth. |
| **2** | **Novice** | Basically correct but poorly structured, generic, or fails to connect to any business utility. |
| **1** | **Substandard** | Technically incorrect, overly vague, or provides a bad user experience. |

## How to use this for AI Testing
For every Q&A pair provided, I will:
1.  **Analyze** the response against these 5 pillars.
2.  **Score** each pillar 1-5.
3.  **Synthesize** an "Overall SE Rating."
4.  **Provide Feedback**: Specific "Expert Tips" on how the AI could upgrade the response from "Functional" to "World-Class."
