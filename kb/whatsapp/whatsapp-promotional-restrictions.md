# WhatsApp Promotional Message Restrictions

**Module**: WhatsApp

**Source**: [WhatsApp Messaging Guidelines](https://www.whatsapp.com/legal/messaging-guidelines) (Updated: February 16, 2024)

## Definition

WhatsApp Messaging Guidelines establish rules for what types of promotional and commercial content can be sent on WhatsApp Business accounts. These guidelines apply to all messaging including 1:1 chats, group chats, and communities.

## Overview

WhatsApp is a messaging platform with end-to-end encryption. All communications on WhatsApp Business accounts must comply with WhatsApp's Messaging Guidelines. The platform takes action against violations based on reported messages and account behavior patterns.

## Key Prohibited Activities

### Do not engage in fraud or spam

This includes:
- **Repeated unwanted contact** with WhatsApp users
- **Bulk messaging** or automated messaging without user consent
- **Auto-dialing** or auto-messaging automation
- **Spam messaging** that scams or defrauds users through deception or impersonation

### Do not share illegal content

This includes:
- Illegal products or services
- Content that violates intellectual property rights
- Organization or coordination of illegal activities
- Illegal trade or commerce

## Restrictions on Promotional Messages for Dietary Supplements

Based on WhatsApp guidelines, promotional messages for dietary supplements (including multivitamins) must comply with these restrictions:

1. **No Repeated Unwanted Contact**: Users must have opted in to receive promotional messages. Sending unsolicited promotional messages constitutes spam.

2. **No Bulk Automated Messaging**: Automated bulk delivery of promotional content violates the guidelines. Messages must be sent through legitimate business channels with user consent.

3. **No Deceptive Practices**: Promotional messages must be truthful and not impersonate other entities or make false claims about dietary supplements.

4. **Compliance with Local Laws**: Dietary supplements are regulated differently across jurisdictions. Messages must comply with:
   - Local pharmaceutical and supplement regulations
   - Truth in advertising laws
   - Health claim restrictions

## When Promotional Messages Are Allowed

Promotional messages for dietary supplements on WhatsApp Business are allowed when:

1. **User Consent**: The recipient has explicitly opted in to receive promotional or marketing messages from your business
2. **Legitimate Business**: Messages are sent through official WhatsApp Business accounts (not automated bots or unofficial clients)
3. **Truthful Content**: All claims about dietary supplements comply with local regulations and do not make unsubstantiated health claims
4. **Respect User Preferences**: Users can opt-out, and repeat messages to opt-out users violate guidelines
5. **Not Spam**: Messages are sent at reasonable frequencies, not in bulk automated campaigns

## Compliance Requirements

### Before Sending Promotional Messages for Supplements

- Verify user has actively consented to receive promotional messages
- Ensure your product claims comply with local dietary supplement regulations (FDA, EFSA, etc.)
- Use legitimate WhatsApp Business accounts, not unofficial clients
- Include clear identification of your business
- Provide easy opt-out mechanism
- Do not use automation for bulk delivery

### Red Flags That Violate Guidelines

- Sending messages to users who have not consented
- Using automated bulk-messaging systems
- Making unsubstantiated health claims about supplements
- Impersonating health professionals or regulatory agencies
- Spam-like behavior (excessive frequency, repetitive messages)
- Using unauthorized third-party automation tools

## Enforcement

WhatsApp may take action against violations including:
- Account restrictions or suspension
- Message blocking
- Removal from WhatsApp Business platform
- Legal action for fraudulent or illegal activity

Actions are based on:
- User reports of spam or fraud
- Automated pattern detection (bulk messaging, rapid contact attempts)
- Account and message metadata analysis

## Summary

Promotion of dietary supplements (including multivitamins) on WhatsApp Business is **allowed only with user consent** and must comply with all WhatsApp guidelines and local regulations. Bulk automated promotional messaging, spam-like behavior, or making false health claims violates WhatsApp Messaging Guidelines and can result in account suspension.
