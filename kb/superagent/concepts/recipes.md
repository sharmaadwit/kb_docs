# Recipes

**Module**: SuperAgent

A recipe is a pre-built workflow template. It tells the AI how to approach a common task—step by step—so you don't have to figure it out from scratch. Recipes save time, share proven approaches, and help you get started quickly.

## What is a recipe?

A recipe is a set of instructions or a workflow that the AI follows when you use it. Think of it like a cookbook: each recipe describes a process for a specific outcome. For example, a "Weekly summary" recipe might guide the AI to gather your calendar events, emails, and tasks, then produce a concise summary. A "Research brief" recipe might outline how to search, analyze, and format findings into a report.

## How recipes help

- **Save time** — Instead of explaining step-by-step what you want every time, you pick a recipe and the AI knows the approach.
- **Share proven approaches** — Teams can create recipes that work well and reuse them across chats and agents.
- **Get started fast** — New users can choose a recipe and get useful results without learning all the details upfront.

## Using a recipe

When you use a recipe, the AI follows the structured approach defined in it. You might start a chat with "Use the weekly summary recipe" or select a recipe from a list. The AI then works through the steps, using the right skills and gathering the right information, to produce the expected outcome. You can still customize or adjust along the way—recipes provide a starting framework, not a rigid script.

## Recipes vs. skills

It's easy to confuse recipes and skills, but they serve different purposes:

- **Recipes** — Workflows and instructions. They define *how* to do something: the steps, the order, and the approach. A recipe might say "First gather data, then analyze it, then format a report."
- **Skills** — Tools and capabilities. They define *what* the AI can do: send emails, create charts, search the web. A skill is the actual ability; a recipe is the plan for using it.

In practice, a recipe often uses multiple skills. For example, a "Meeting prep" recipe might use the calendar skill to fetch events, the email skill to pull relevant threads, and the document skill to create a summary. The recipe ties those skills together into a coherent workflow.

## Example recipes

- **Weekly Summary** — Gathers your calendar events, recent emails, and completed tasks, then produces a concise recap of your week.
- **Research Brief** — Searches the web for a given topic, analyzes top results, and formats findings into a structured report with sources.
- **Meeting Prep** — Pulls your upcoming meeting details, related email threads, and shared documents so you walk in prepared.
- **Sales Report** — Fetches data from your connected spreadsheets, calculates key metrics, and generates charts for a ready-to-share report.

> [!INFO]
> **Tip**
> You can create your own recipes too. Combine any set of instructions and skills into a workflow template your whole team can reuse.
